Our History

Kenya National Association of Social Workers (KNASW) is the professional body of social workers in Kenya. The body was founded in 1962, thereafter due to its growth, demand and clarity of membership the body was registered with the Registrar of Societies under section 10 of Societies Act on 25th November, 1997. The association is a member of the Professional Societies of East Africa and is affiliated to the International Federation of Social Workers (IFSW) and collaborates closely with Commonwealth Organization for Social Workers (COSW). Initially, the membership of the association included the entire Kenya social welfare workforce involved in humanitarian work including those in collective, rehabilitative and curative services. In essence this brought together nurses, probation officers, community development workers, counselors, social activists, paralegals and academia. KNASW is one, among other over 20 national associations in Africa who form IFSW African Region. Other regions are: Asia / Pacific, Europe, Latin America and North America (US& Canada), all the five regions from IFSW global whose headquarters is in Berne, Switzerland.
