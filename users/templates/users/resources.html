Chairperson Remarks

Our History
The Kenya National Association of Social Workers (KNASW) is committed to providing valuable resources and services to its members to support their professional growth and development. These resources and services may include:

1. Professional Development Materials: The KNASW offers members access to a wide range of professional development materials, such as research articles, publications, and educational resources. These resources enable members to stay updated on best practices, emerging trends, and relevant research in the field of social work.

2. Online Member Portal: Members may have access to an exclusive online member portal, which serves as a hub for information, news, and resources related to the social work profession. The portal may include a member directory, discussion forums, access to webinars, and other interactive features that facilitate knowledge sharing and collaboration among members.

3. Continuing Education Opportunities: The KNASW may organize and provide opportunities for members to engage in continuing education activities, such as workshops, seminars, and training programs. These events are designed to enhance members' knowledge and skills, keeping them abreast of advancements in the field and supporting their professional growth.

4. Professional Support: The association may offer various forms of professional support to its members. This could include mentorship programs, where experienced social workers provide guidance and support to early-career professionals. Additionally, the KNASW may provide a platform for members to seek advice, share challenges, and access resources related to their professional practice.

Mentoring and Networking Opportunities: The KNASW recognizes the importance of mentoring and networking for professional development and career advancement. The association may facilitate mentoring and networking opportunities for its members in the following ways:

Mentorship Programs: The KNASW may establish formal mentorship programs, connecting experienced social workers with early-career professionals. These programs foster supportive relationships, allowing mentors to share their knowledge, expertise, and experiences while mentees receive guidance and support in navigating their careers.

Networking Events: The association may organize networking events such as conferences, seminars, or social gatherings, where members can connect with other professionals in the field. These events provide opportunities to exchange ideas, build professional relationships, and collaborate on projects or initiatives.

Online Networking Platforms: The KNASW may offer online networking platforms, such as professional social media groups or forums, where members can interact, share insights, and seek advice from a broader network of social work professionals. These platforms facilitate connections and collaboration beyond geographical boundaries.

Volunteer Opportunities: The KNASW may provide members with volunteer opportunities to actively engage in community service and contribute to the betterment of society. These opportunities can include:

Community Outreach Programs: The association may organize or partner with organizations to conduct community outreach programs where members can volunteer their time and skills to support individuals, families, and communities in need. This could involve activities such as organizing workshops, providing counseling services, or participating in community development projects.

Advocacy Initiatives: Members may have the chance to volunteer for advocacy initiatives aimed at promoting social justice and addressing social issues. This could involve participating in advocacy campaigns, engaging in public awareness activities, or contributing to policy development efforts.

Collaboration with Nonprofit Organizations: The KNASW may establish partnerships with nonprofit organizations that align with the association's mission and goals. Through these partnerships, members can volunteer their expertise and contribute to the work of these organizations, addressing social challenges and making a positive impact in the community.

By providing member resources and services, mentoring and networking opportunities, and volunteer opportunities, the KNASW supports its members in their professional journey, fosters collaboration and knowledge-sharing, and encourages active engagement in community service. These initiatives enhance members' professional development, broaden their professional networks, and empower them to make a meaningful difference in the lives of individuals and communities they serve.
